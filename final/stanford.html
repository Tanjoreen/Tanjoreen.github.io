The Big Game between Stanford and Cal is a historic college football rivalry that dates back to 1892, making it one of the oldest and most enduring matchups in the sport. The annual clash between the Stanford Cardinal and the California Golden Bears is marked by intense competition, passionate fan bases, and a deep-seated rivalry that transcends the football field. Adding a unique dimension to this rivalry is the Stanford Axe tradition. In 1899, a group of Stanford students stole a blue and gold axe from a California cheering section, sparking a tradition that endures to this day. The stolen axe became the coveted prize awarded to the winner of the Big Game, symbolizing the triumph of one university over the other. This longstanding tradition has added a distinctive and spirited element to the Stanford-Cal rivalry, making the Big Game more than just a football matchup but a cultural and historical celebration of collegiate competition.
